Hi! I'm Anuj

I want to learn, build, and contribute to the future — and maybe make some weird and wonderful things along the way.

I’m a quantum physicist with a fascination for how mathematics can help us understand — and even shape — the quantum world. I studied at University College London (UCL), where I focused on exploring the mathematical foundations of quantum computing and other areas of quantum physics. These days, I spend most of my time thinking about how abstract math can lead to real, exciting breakthroughs in quantum tech.

Beyond research, I like to explore side projects that blend science, creativity, and engineering — especially in robotics and simulation. I’m always curious about how things work (and how they might work better). I also enjoy reading, watching films, and I will occasionally go on a rant in my blog things that catch my attention.

I’ve grown up all over — Singapore, Vienna, India, Zurich, and London — and that global perspective has shaped how I think, learn, and collaborate. I love meeting people from different backgrounds and finding unexpected connections between disciplines.
